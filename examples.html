D3E1 - Priciples of Interactions

Human interactions with machines can be understood through various examples where the degree of control and influence differs, ranging from human-driven to machine-driven processes, and some that lie in between. Let's explore this concept using three examples: an RC car, a Total Artificial Heart (TAH), and a PlayStation console.

Human Influence - RC Car:

An RC (remote-controlled) car represents a scenario where human influence is predominant. In this case, the human operates the car using a remote control, dictating every movement—whether it's speeding up, slowing down, turning left, or right. The car has no autonomy; it only responds to the direct commands given by the human operator. This interaction is a clear example of humans having full control, where the machine (RC car) is a tool that extends human capability without contributing any decision-making or independent action.

Machine Influence - Total Artificial Heart (TAH):

A Total Artificial Heart (TAH), which replaces the lower chambers of the heart (ventricles) and maintains blood circulation, exemplifies a scenario where machine influence is significant. The TAH is designed to operate autonomously, without requiring constant human input to function. It makes real-time adjustments to maintain proper blood flow and pressure, ensuring the patient’s survival. Although human input was crucial in designing, programming, and installing the TAH, once implanted, the machine largely takes over the vital role that the human heart once played. This example highlights a situation where the machine's influence is critical, and it operates with a level of autonomy, significantly impacting the patient's life.

Equal Influence - PlayStation:

The interaction between a person and a PlayStation console is a balanced example where human and machine influence are equally significant. The human player brings creativity, strategy, and decision-making into the gaming experience. They control the characters, make choices, and determine the gameplay's direction. On the other hand, the PlayStation console processes these inputs and generates responses through the game's programming. The console’s processing power and game design influence how the game unfolds, providing challenges, responses, and scenarios that the player interacts with. Here, human and machine are in a dynamic partnership, where both influence the experience equally.

Exploring the Balance Between Human and Machine Influence:

These examples illustrate how human interaction with machines varies based on the level of control and autonomy involved. In the case of the RC car, humans have full control, with the machine simply following commands. With the TAH, the machine takes over a critical function, minimizing the need for human input, thereby highlighting the machine’s influence. The PlayStation represents a middle ground, where both human creativity and machine processing contribute equally to the experience.

In modern society, as technology evolves, the balance between human and machine influence continues to shift. Understanding this balance is key to navigating our increasingly intertwined relationship with technology, ensuring that machines enhance human capabilities while still allowing for meaningful human input and interaction.
